THE KHELO INDIA SCHEME

Sports and fitness hold immense value in our lives, promoting qualities such as teamwork, strategic thinking, leadership, goal setting, and risk-taking. They contribute significantly to individual well-being, social health, and the strength of a nation. Recognizing the vital role of sports in our overall development, India has shown steady progress in recent years.

To showcase this potential on a global scale, it is crucial to inspire and provide exceptional infrastructure and training to young talents. Fostering a strong spirit of sports participation will enable players to unleash their true potential, paving the way for India to become a sports powerhouse.

The Khleo India program aims to revitalize the sports culture in India from the grassroots level by establishing a robust framework for all sports played in the country, with the vision of positioning India as a prominent sporting nation. Talented athletes identified by the High-Powered Committee in priority sports disciplines will receive annual financial assistance of INR 5 lakh per year for a duration of 8 years.

THE FIT INDIA MOVEMENT

The FIT INDIA was launched on August 29, 2019, on the 114th birth anniversary of Major Dhyan Chand, India's hockey legend, by the Honourable Prime Minister. Its purpose is to make fitness an integral part of our daily lives. The movement aims to popularize the culture of sports and fitness in the country by encouraging people to prioritize fitness in their day-to-day routines. It strives to create awareness about the importance of physical activity and inspire individuals to lead healthier lifestyles.

Fit India's mission is to promote behavioural changes and encourage a more active lifestyle. The campaign is designed as a four-year plan, with each year dedicated to a different fitness-related topic. The first year will emphasize physical fitness, followed by a focus on healthy eating habits, an environmentally friendly lifestyle, and disease prevention in the second, third, and fourth years, respectively.

Fit India aims to achieve its mission by implementing various initiatives and hosting events that align with the following objectives:

  • To promote fitness as easy, fun and free.
  • To spread awareness on fitness and various physical activities that promote fitness through focused campaigns.
  • To encourage indigenous sports.
  • To make fitness reach every school, college/university, panchayat/village, etc.
  • To create a platform for citizens of India to share information, drive awareness and encourage sharing of personal fitness stories.

Target Olympic Podium Scheme

The Target Olympic Podium Scheme (TOPS) is a flagship initiative of the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, designed to support India's top athletes in achieving success at the highest level. In April 2018, the scheme underwent a revamp, introducing a dedicated technical support team to manage the "TOPS" athletes and provide comprehensive assistance. The scheme allocates additional resources to these athletes' preparations, with the aim of increasing their chances of winning Olympic medals.

The Mission Olympic Cell (MOC) was created as a specialized entity to provide support and assistance to the athletes chosen under the TOP Scheme. Chaired by the Director General of the Sports Authority of India (SAI), the MOC holds regular meetings attended by representatives from National Sports Federations (NSFs), SAI Project Officers, and other members. These meetings serve to assess athlete proposals and periodically review their progress and performances. The committee's recommendations determine whether athletes are eligible for inclusion, continuation, or exclusion from the TOP Scheme.

Sports Authority of India

The Operations Division of SAI (Sports Authority of India) is responsible for implementing various sports promotional schemes aimed at identifying and nurturing talented athletes across different age groups to achieve excellence at the national and international levels. These schemes are implemented through SAI's Regional Centres located in Bengaluru, Kolkata, Gandhinagar, Kandivali (Mumbai), Bhopal, Sonepat, Lucknow, Chandigarh, Guwahati, and Imphal.

Additionally, SAI's Academic Wings located at NS NIS (Patiala) and LNCPE (Thiruvananthapuram) play a role in implementing these schemes. The Regional Centres and Academic Wings are supported by well-developed sports science facilities in Patiala, Bengaluru, and Kolkata, with ongoing efforts to upgrade facilities in other centres as well.

The Sports Promotional Schemes are

  • NATIONAL CENTRES OF EXCELLENCE
  • SAI TRAINING CENTER(STC)
  • EXTENSION CENTERS OF STC
  • NATIONAL SPORTS TALENT CONTEST
